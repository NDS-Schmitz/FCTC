From Cops To Coders

From Cops To Coders (FCTC) is a free program for current and recently former law enforcement officers to learn the skills needed to leave law enforcement and switch to software engineering. Law Enforcement Officers often think that they have no path to transfer out of law enforcement. That simply is not true.

FCTC is here to help current and former law enforcement learn the skills they need to transfer out of law enforcement. This can be for free without having to get a degree. Often times people who start this path can be hired as a software engineer in 6-12 months. See "FAQ" and "How To Get Started" for more information. Also see below for why software engineering. This website was built by cops who are currently working their way through this program.

Why learn Software Engineering?

Job Growth

According to the United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, Software Engineering is projected to grow its labor force by 25% in the next decade! It also shows only having a 3% unemployment rate which is lower than the US Average. A simple search through indeed.com for remote software engineer came up with 46,000 jobs!

Bureau of Labor Statistics

Pay

The median pay for software engineers is $109,000 per year. There are also plenty of opportunities to supplement income with contract work.

Education Requirements

None! The great thing about software engineering is there are no formal education requirements! Many software engineers are self taught professionals. There is so much information available online which is where Cops to Coders comes in. This career is one of continuous learning to stay up to date with the latest formats and languages.

Remote Work

With the availability of programs such as Teams, and Zoom there are becoming more and more opportunities to work from home. Some of these employers even let you choose your hours!

Job Satisfaction

Software engineering is consistently at the top of all job satisfaction surveys! There are a lot of upward mobility opportunities, low to average stress levels, and a ton of flexibility! You'll never be sitting alone in your car for Christmas.

Creativity

Have you ever come up with a great idea for an app? With the baseline knowledge of coding you will be able to easily learn the programming languages you desire and watch your ideas come to life.

Community

In the software engineering community there are tons of opportunities for collaboration and social events. There are many conferences throughout the world every single year for the tech community. There are also many local groups that have their own meet-ups and get togethers. One of the most popular websites in the community, GitHub, is all about collaboration and sharing your ideas with others.

Improvement

Another great aspect of software engineering is that there is always room to improve. You will constantly find better ways to code better and learn. You never stop learning when it comes to software engineering.
